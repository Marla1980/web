Digital video // 41’ // 2026 // EMPELT grant for artistic research

What are the greatest challenges facing the art museum of the future? Are they architectural? Technological? Related to conservation? Sustainability? Public engagement? What is the role of museums in the 21st century? Do we continue to cling to the obsolete white cube? How will political changes and the neoliberal drift affect museum management? And the use of Artificial Intelligence? How can a museum—which houses works from the past—face the future? What is our relationship with the concept of heritage? Can a museum transform the hierarchical, patriarchal, and colonial structures that underpin it? Does it make sense for museums to continually expand their collections in a world where natural resources are increasingly scarce? All these questions and many others arise when we discuss art museums and their uncertain future. Based on the specific case study of the MNAC (National Art Museum of Catalonia), which will expand with a new venue in 2029 (the Victoria Eugenia Palace), the video essay The Museum of the Future reflects on art and the future of museums in an era when uncertainty has taken root in our lives.
