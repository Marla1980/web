Between 1966 and 1977, the Canadian singer and composer Glenn Gould recorded three experimental radio programs that formed the so-called Trilogy of Solitude, made up of The Idea of North, The Latecomers and The Quiet in the Land, experimental works that Gould defined as "The closest thing to an autobiographical statement broadcast by radio" and in which he applied the technique of counterpoint to the protagonist narrative voices, which reflected on the effects of loneliness and isolation.

Notes on a Fugue reinterprets the radio programs by Glenn Gould and proposes a video installation with three synchronized screens that uses the structure of a fugue to reflect on loneliness in twenty first century and how it affects three generations of women.
