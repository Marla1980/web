(From the series: Annotations to an Eisegesis)

Performance, single-channel video, text, e-mails // 59’48’’ // 2014
Huídas. La ficción como rigor. Fabra i Coats Contemporary Arts Centre, Barcelona

In the year 2002, british writer Harold B Morrison published the book The Archetypal Writer; the first novel of a young promise, an ironic and corrosive reflection on stereotypes in the world of literature. A few months later, the small publishing house that published the book was hopelessly bankrupt. The economic problems became unsustainable.  Of Morrison’s book had been made two thousand copies, but a fire on December 24 2002, burned most of them, still undistributed. 30 boxes stacked in a corner of the warehouse devoured by the flames. Only a few libraries had the novel for sale. Morrison died in a trafic accident and the possibilities of reedition of the novel disappeared.

Using some excerpts from the book The Archetypal Writer, F(r)ictions relates elements such as literature, writing, reading, art or cinema; questioning the boundaries between reality and fiction.
