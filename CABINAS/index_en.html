In 1876, Alexander Graham Bell patented the telephone (patent number 174,465), although in 2002 the Congress of the United States of America recognised that its true inventor was Antonio Meucci, who in 1854 built a first prototype whose patent was not formalised due to financial difficulties. The telephone became one of the inventions that would revolutionize communication technologies. In the following years, telephones progressively reached homes and also the streets, thus becoming a compulsory universal service for decades. In 2021, the new state telecommunications law was approved, which will lead to the future disappearance of public telephones from the urban landscape. It is estimated that approximately half a thousand public phone booths remain in the city of Barcelona, although at present they are no more than fossils out of service, located on the pavements and ignored by the majority of passers-by.

174465 makes a photographic cartography of these booths, which thus become an unexpected element on which to focus our attention. Alongside the images of the booths, an archive of transcriptions of telephone conversations that may (or may not) have taken place at some point in the past.
