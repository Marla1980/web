After the Napoleonic wars, between 1816 and 1830, German philosopher and military Carl von Clausewitz wrote On War (Vom Kriege), one of the most well-known works on strategy and military tactics that would end up becoming mandatory reading in many military academies. The work consists of 8 books: On the Nature of War, On the Theory of War, Of Strategy in General, The Combat, Military Forces, Defence, The Attack and Plan of War. This series consists on works made from these texts that raises a critical reflection on the way in which different societies apply strategies of the warlike context in daily environments to exercise power, whether in a physical, psychological, moral, emotional, economic way, etc.
