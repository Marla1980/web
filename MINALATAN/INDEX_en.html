(From the series: Annotations to an Eisegesis)

Digital vídeo // 6’ // 2020 // Finalist Visual Arts Award Vila de Santanyí Francisco Bernareggi, Casa de Cultura Ses Cases Noves, Mallorca

In 1943, the writer and philosopher Ayn Rand published The Fountainhead, one of her best-known novels and a plea for individualism. The Fountainhead tells the story of Howard Roark, a young architect who has to fight against prejudice and social conventions to defend his innovative work. Thanks to this and other novels, Rand became one of the main defenders of objectivism, individualism and laissez-faire capitalism, influencing with her ideas the work of liberal economists such as Milton Friedman. In 1949, King Vidor directed a film adaptation of The Fountainhead starring Gary Cooper. In the main sequence of the film, the protagonist, brought to trial, delivers a final speech in favor of individualism and objectivism advocated by Rand (screenwriter of the film). Unable to understand a single word of the speech, Gary Cooper learned it by heart and repeated it on camera without being fully aware of what he was saying. In Minalatan I return to that sequence, rearrange the shots in reverse order (from last to first), blur the entire image except for Gary Cooper's face and replace his voice with the voice of Daniel, one of the voices of Macintosh computer.
