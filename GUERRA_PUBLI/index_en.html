De la guerra (notas al pie para el momento presente) is an essay that takes as its starting point On War, a manual of military strategy written by Carl von Clausewitz after the Napoleonic Wars. Although two hundred years have passed, the strategies proposed by Clausewitz are still valid; what's more, contemporary neoliberalism has completely assimilated them, making them part of our society, so that these strategies are used not only in the context of war but also in various aspects of our daily lives. Through the selection and analysis of 30 works from the Hamaca videoart archive, in this essay I reflect on how the strategies defined by Clausewitz in On War remain, today, more relevant than ever.
