(From the series: Thoughts on the Characteristics the Literature of the Future Should Have, or Not)

Single-channel video // 12’ // 2016 // Open Studios Glogauair, Berlín, 2016 // XVIII Concurso Internacional Encuentros de Arte Contemporáneo, Alicante, 2018
Versos y versiones: de la palabra a la imagen, Galileo Cultural Center, Madrid, 2022

In an interview in 1932 with Ramón María del Valle-Inclán, the interviewer asks the writer how will the literature in the year 2000 be like. “I cannot imagine it and I’m not even interested in how it could be. I neither couldn’t control my opinion and no one could say if I am right or wrong. If I could know it, if I could guess how the future literature will be, I would be writing it right now.” responds the writer stroking his beard.

This question, “How will the literature of the future be like?” is the leitmotiv of a series of works that I’ve been making since 2011. I know that this is an unasnwered question, but in spite of it, I have been researching for years the hypotheses and theories of many writers about it (Enrique Vila-Matas, Witold Gombrowicz, Raymond Carver, G. K. Chesterton, Italo Calvino, James Joyce…), realizing that all of them are true somehow, even when they contradict each other. Unable to get the one and only answer to this question, the only possible reaction is to keep on researching and investigate the past looking for clues and statements about it. Like those of Valle-Inclán, who admits his inability to give a concrete answer.
