On October 6, 2019, I found more than a hundred wedding photographs among the remains of a flea market in Belgrade: color digital images showing a middle-class Serbian wedding. Wedding images like these are the paradigm of canonical happiness, since in them no one can afford not to look happy. But lying on the ground in the street and trampled by passers-by, they took on other connotations. Such images seen in a context like this one become the metaphor of a frustrated dream, that of marriage as a supposedly unquestionable and everlasting sacrament that lasts throughout the years. I observe the images, the faces, the smiles of the wedding guests. In fact, it could be any wedding, since in all of them -or at least, in most of them-, the same gestures, the same poses are repeated. I decide to break them down to produce a single large image that somehow comes close to abstraction to represent that frustrated dream; a chromatic landscape in which all the people photographed cease to have their own entity and become part of a whole.
