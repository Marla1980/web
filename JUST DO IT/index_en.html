Just Do It!

(From the series: Annotations to an Eisegesis)

Digital video // 30' // 2019
Shadows of Language Travel at the Speed of Light, Württembergischer Kunstverein Stuttgart, 2019
Cultura Online al CCCC, Centre del Carme, 2020

This video proposes a reflection on the possibilities of staying out of the capitalist system or, at least, be critical of it and aware of its functioning. A fast forward high angle shot in which I appear solving a 1000 piece puzzle where we can see the richest people in the world, a sort of graduation picture of those who society has highlighted above the rest. Sud­denly, in the foreground, an unexpected element appears: the actor Shia LaBeouf making his motivational speech Just do it, “the most intense motivational speech of all times”, a grotesque parody of all those speeches who claims to be able to change people’s lives. La Beouf performed in 2015 in collaboration with students from Central Saint Martins. In this performance, the actor made various speeches proposed by the students in front of a green chroma background, one of which (the above mentioned Just do it) progressively became a viral phenomena with more than 27 million views on YouTube. By publishing these images on the Internet, La Beouf opened the possibility for viewers to download the video, decontextualize these images and relocate them in any other context, giving rise to endless parodies and modifications.The idea of this project comes from a concern that has gradually appeared in my life in recent years. Could we question the system without being contradictory to our own behavior? Can we be co­herent with all our words and actions? Faced with my inability to achieve it, the only possible reaction is to manifest this impotence through the realization of an artwork.
