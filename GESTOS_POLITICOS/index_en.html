(From the series: Annotations to an Eiségesis)

Single-channel video // 8’22’' // 2017 // Music: Néstor Català
Finalist in Obra Abierta International Visual Arts Award Fundación Caja Extremadura, 2018.

Project that aims to create an archive of political GIFs in order to analize them. Bearing in mind that non-verbal communication represents aproximately 80% of everything we transmit, then we can understand that all gestures and grimaces made by public figures catch our attention and remain inmortalized by means of a big amount of animated GIFs that repeat constantly this movement until it becomes a new gesture that, deprived of his original meaning and context, it becomes viral image, usually with humorous connotations. But, why are we so obssesed with these gestures? What communicate us the movement of a hand, the intensity of a gaze or a way of walking?  Could it be a certain feeling of self-identification? Or maybe it’s like the need to ridicule the other person?
