The humorous book El arte de perder el tiempo (The Art of Wasting Time), written by Noel Claras√≥ Daudi in 1946, serves as a starting point for a homonymous performance that reflects on the possible reasons for wasting time and the probable consequences of doing so in the 21st century. Is it still possible to waste time in a society that forces us to be more and more productive every day? What does "wasting time" really mean? According to the Royal Spanish Academy, wasting time means "not taking advantage of it, or not doing in it what you could or should. Working in vain." What the definition does not specify is what we should do with our time. Do many things at once? Or focus our energies on just one thing? Always be in a hurry and do everything quickly even at the risk of making a mistake? Or keep calm and do things slowly but carefully? Be productive and obey the dictates of the prevailing neoliberalism? Or enjoy time to the fullest without caring about productivity? Talk about that inevitable passage of time? Or keep silent and just watch it go by?
