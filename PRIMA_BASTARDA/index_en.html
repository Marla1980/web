Giving a new twist to the six points of separation theory, The Bastard Cousin of Stephen Dedalus seeks to establish points of contact between Stephen Dedalus (James Joyce´s alter ego) and R (the artist’s pseudonym). Themes as adolescence, self portrayal or biographical fiction lead to a sarcastic tragicomedy in five acts which, among other things, constitutes a metalinguistic study of the way we tell stories. The main interest lies not so much in the feasibility of the possible links between the two individuals as in the strategies used to establish them and demonstrating that the way in which we assimilate memories (or not) conditions the way we tell biographical stories.
