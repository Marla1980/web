In the early 1960s, the Eastman Kodak company developed the Super-8 film format, an evolution of the 8 mm film that appeared in the 1930s. From then until the appearance of video in the 1980s, millions of families used Super-8 cameras to record the moments they wanted to remember. But what drives people to record only certain moments in a life? Why do rituals, expressions, kinesics and gestures repeat themselves over the years in people from different parts of the world? Do we intend to collect personal memory or contribute to create a kind of involuntary collective memory? Are our memories a pre-learned staging? Can the elements that are part of a constellation decide the position they occupy in it? Ext Day. A Common Family Smiles is a video essay composed with a selection of film fragments from the Centre de la Imatge Mas Iglesias that reflects on all those collective rituals carried out as a family that we often perpetuate in front of the cameras.
