The flight of a hummingbird fans the flames of a pyre of burning frames proposes a reflection on how the human species thinks about the future, specifically through the cinema. The first part of the exhibition is made up of 365 images. Images that time and (bad) memory have defocused and in which only one phrase lasts. Images that actually are, or rather were, movie frames that I saw at some point in my life. Through the creation of a fictional character that is shown only through his own voice-over, the video reflects on various aspects of contemporary society starting from a future as fictitious as imminent. Finally, in addition to the 365 images and the video projection, we observe 12 larger black and white images. Images that refer to a past, but are accompanied by texts that reflect on the potential of cinema to shape infinite futures.
