Digital video // 19’ // 2026

In 1909, French folklorist and ethnographer Arnold Van Gennep introduced the concept of liminality in his book The Rites of Passage, referring to the possibility of being on a threshold, between something that has gone and something that is yet to come. In 2026, 117 years later, this concept has become popular and so-called liminal spaces (both existing and non-existing) have flooded the internet. Furthermore, the unstable social and political situation has made uncertainty our normal state. We have had to get used to constant change, to an unpredictable society that is constantly fluctuating, to the constant feeling of not knowing our place in the world. The video Liminal State explores a series of liminal spaces created using generative AI while reflecting (through autofiction) on some significant events of 2025.
