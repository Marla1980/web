The lectures:
In 1985, Harvard University invited Italo Calvino to give six lectures. Calvino decided that he would talk about the features that literature should keep in the XXIst Century. Unfortunately, he died one week before giving these lectures and only wrote five of them: Lightness, Quickness, Exactitude, Visibility and Multiplicity. He never finished the last one: Consistency.
