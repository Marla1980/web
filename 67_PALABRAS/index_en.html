(From the series: Thoughts on the Characteristics the Literature of the Future Should Have, or Not)

Video installation
Single-channel video, framed puzzle
18’37’’
Dimensions variable
Qué podem?, Can Felipa, Barcelona
2014

In 1986 The Art of the Novel, a compilation of essays of the Txech writer Milan Kundera, is published. The sixth chapter of the book –67 Words– is a glossary of recurrent terms in Kundera’s literature accompanied by a subjective definition given by the writer. In 67 Words, I take these terms to reinterpret them and use them again, creating a story where a ficticious character reflects on the features that the future literature should –or shouldn’t–have. Thereby, I try to get my own speech based on the combination of alien elements, trying to reinterpret historical facts taking as a starting point non official facts. Therefore, these words are shown together with some videos belonging to Prelinguer Archives, mainly amateur videos from the 40’s (many of them shot by unknown authors).
