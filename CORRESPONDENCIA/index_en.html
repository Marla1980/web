Installation // 20 glass jars of 17 x 8 cm, 20 letters by diferent writers // 2024

Why does a writer write? To express him/herself? To be read? To do therapy? Because he/she cannot avoid it? One of the purposes of a writer when he writes a book is, generally, to be published and/or read by a more or less wide public, although... is the same true for private correspondence? Obviously not, but despite this, countless writers' letters have been published (almost always after their death). Is it fair that anyone can read these letters if they were addressed to someone in particular? Is the literary value of such correspondence more important than the respect for the private life of writers? In Correspondence, I select 20 private letters written by various writers (Sylvia Plath, Henry Miller,
Hannah Arendt, TS Eliot, Alejandra Pizarnik, John Keats, Anais Nin...) and insert all the words of each letter, cut out one by one, in a glass jar, leaving only the
first sentence at the top of the letter visible.
