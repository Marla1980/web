Perder teorías tells the story of Samuel Riba (Enrique Vila-Matas’s heteronym), who wrote a literary theory to explain the features that future literature should have. Those five features are: literature as an advancing clock, “intertextuality”, connections with high poetry, predominance of style (not the plot) and the conciousness of a ruinous moral landscape. Vila-Matas used these five features in the writing of Dublinesca. I use Dublinesca to destroy itself and create a new work of art according to these five features.
